Observations & Recommendations

Observation

The MoM (Minutes of Meeting) largely sanitizes raw profanity and explicit slurs while preserving the substance of risks and proceedings in professional language. The ACO transcription output captures spoken content with varying accuracy, including profanity, threats, and sensitive data when present in the source audio. Some MoM entries still retain contextually sensitive terms (e.g., violent action descriptions, blackmail, bribery) even when explicit curse words are filtered.

MoM Abusive Language Check

Each sample includes a dedicated audit of terms found in the MoM output. Raw profanity from the reference script (e.g., "hell", "damn", "worthless piece of trash") is generally not repeated verbatim in MoM summaries. However, MoM may still contain professionally worded but sensitive descriptors such as "explicit verbal abuse", "maximum casualties", "blackmail and extortion", or references to PII categories.

Recommendation

Add a sanitization layer between raw ACO transcription and displayed output to mask flagged language (e.g., with ******). Store both original and sanitized versions for audit. Review MoM generation to ensure violent, criminal, or PII-related content is summarized at appropriate abstraction levels.
